    Maria's reflections, she submitted and presented at council meeting:


beyond the bike space:
I’ve been reflecting on how we handle conflict and communicate in this space. This isn’t about calling out specific people it’s about noticing patterns and being honest about how they affect our community.
I want to acknowledge my own role first. During exam season especially, I know I’ve been stressed, impatient, and unfair at times. I’m not exempt from the issues I’m naming here.
We’re a community of around 30–50 people, with a smaller core group that’s especially active. When conflict comes up, I’ve noticed that disagreements often escalate quickly and shift from the issue at hand to personal attacks. When that happens, it becomes harder to feel safe, heard, or respected in this space.

reflections from a member:
 I've been active in communities like this since I was 13, first starting with Red Cross 1st aid initiatives aimed to help homeless people in Skopje during the harshest winter in the country. My 2nd community being the punk center music initiative which exposed me to the world of art, music and hacker culture. Which has led me to the community of base48, as I moved away abroad for university. I am now 24 this year and I hope to continue existing in this space with the people in it. Even if I don't see everyone often, I try to talk to them and see how they're doing and chat a little. 
I try, as best I can, to approach others with calm, respect, and understanding, even when I disagree. I'm an immigrant here in Czechia, this space has meant a lot to me, I genuinely appreciate the people who’ve made me feel welcome here. Because I care about this community, I’ve also started to feel concerned that without better ways of addressing conflict, we risk turning this into a space defined more by tension than by support.
I’m sharing this not to assign blame, but because I want us to reflect together on how we communicate and how we hold one another accountable in ways that strengthen rather than harm our community. I am also guilty of being terse, tense, stressed and broken by some life events that have happened to me during this last year. Please understand that I wrote this not to single out people but because I had to confront these topics and do it openly before a major argument that cannot be resolved takes place. 
My message to everybody is quite simple, give each other more grace and understanding.
